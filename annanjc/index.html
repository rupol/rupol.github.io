what

Fun with friends on the shortest night of the year!

Come join us for a weekend of camping and fun on the shortest night of the year! The solstice is a time for reflection and mindfulness, and what better way to mark the passage of time than spending it outdoors with friends.

After a fun night spent around the bonfire in our backyard this past winter solstice, we decided that we needed to do something similar for the summer solstice. Inspired by an annual 'pig roast' campout some friends of friends used to do, and our wedding spent with friends and family in a big group site near Twin Lakes, we decided to book a campsite to share with friends and usher in summertime fun! We're hoping to make this a yearly tradition, so if there's a place you know of that would be awesome to go next year, let's do it.

bring

Colorado weather is notoriously fickle. While it should be sunny and warm, it can snow any time of year up here, so it is always a good idea to be prepared for any temperature. If you're new to camping, make sure you bring clothes that are comfy and warm with layers to shed if it decides to be 80 degrees.

Must haves:

  • tent/tarp/hammock/dead tauntaun
  • sleeping bags/blankies/pillows/other bed stuff
  • warm clothes
  • food and lots of water
  • medications and anything else you need to survive!
  • sunscreen/hat/sunnies

Nice to haves:

  • camping chair
  • sleeping pads/air mattress
  • beverages/preferred substances
  • a cooler to keep those bevvies cold
  • lantern/headlamp
  • doggos and nature cats/ferrets
  • stove/grill and tableware
  • coozies
  • cash for firewood
  • bugspray

Extras:

  • games (the site has volleyball courts!)
  • marshmallows
  • bluetooth speaker
  • canopy
  • any other funn stuff you can think of!
